Multi-variable calculus

Multi-variable calculus \\ Multiple integration examples

  1. Evaluate the integral \[\iint\limits_{R} y \, dA,\] where the integral is taken over the region \(R\) in the plane, bounded by the four curves \[y=(x+1)^2, \, \, x=y-y^3, \, \, x=-1, \,\, y=-1.\] (See this Sage cell for a plot of these boundary curves)

  2. Evaluate \[\iint\limits_{R} 3x \, dA ,\] where the region of integration \(R\) is the region in the upper half-plane bounded by the circles of radii 1 and 2.

  3. Evaluate the integral \[\iint\limits_{D} \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \, dx \, dy ,\] where the integral is taken over the disc \(D\) in the \(xy\)-plane of radius 5.

  4. Evaluate the following triple integral \[ \iiint\limits_{R} \, z \, dx \, dy \, dz ,\] where the integral is taken over the region \(R\) of \(xyz\)-space consisting of the upper-half (\(z \geq 0\)) of the ball of radius 1.
